Administrative Rules (Section A)

Eligibility, conduct, documentation and general rules

Vehicle Eligibility (A2)

New Vehicle Rule

One Year Only

A vehicle competes for one competition year only, starting from Day 1 on-site. Must have a newly manufactured chassis with significant Primary Structure changes vs predecessor.

Chassis Reuse

Apply via FSQD

Submit reuse request before the SES deadline. Approved by Chief Judge — decision is final. May attract Design Event penalty points. DV vehicles may always reuse a previous chassis.

Team Member Requirements (A4)

Age

18+ Years

All team members must be at least 18 years old (A4.2.6).

Student Status

Enrolled Student

Must be enrolled in a degree programme. Graduates remain eligible for 7 months post-graduation.

Driver Licence

Valid Licence

Drivers need a valid, government-issued, photographic driver's licence or a recognised motorsport licence.

IMechE Membership

Required (UK Teams)

UK students must be IMechE members. Student Affiliate is FREE. International students may use their home engineering institution.

No Pro Drivers

Banned

Drivers who have competed for a professional racing team in a national/international series may not drive at any event.

One Team Only

No Dual Teams

A team member may only be part of one team and work on one vehicle (A4.2.1). Penalty: 25 pts.

Key Roles

[EV Only] Electrical System Officer (ESO)

• At least one ESO required per EV team
• Only person who can declare vehicle electrically safe
• Must accompany vehicle whenever moved
• Contactable by phone at all times
• Cannot be a driver unless second ESO is named
• Must have HV training — details in ESO/ESA form

Faculty Advisor

• Must accompany team to the live event
• Official representative of the university
• If absent: must send a signed letter nominating a replacement
• May not design, build or repair any part of the vehicle
• May advise on general engineering theory only

Rules of Conduct — Key Penalties
  • A3.2.1 Failure to follow an official's instruction: –25 points
  • A3.3.1 Argument with or disobedience to any official: Disqualification
  • A3.4.1 Unsportsmanlike conduct: –25 pts. Second violation: Disqualification
  • A3.7.3 Protests must be filed in writing by the Team Leader with a 25-point protest bond (forfeited if rejected)
  • A3.9 Covering/obscuring a competing car: up to –50 pts at Chief Judge's discretion
Insurance (A4.10)
⚠️ Critical Requirement
All teams must have: (1) personal accident insurance for all drivers, and (2) Public Liability Insurance — minimum £15 million. Upload certificate via the competition website. Non-UK participants must also show valid individual health insurance.

Vehicle Configuration Rules (T2)
  • T2.1.2Must be open-wheeled, single seat, open cockpit (formula style) with 4 wheels not in a straight line
  • T2.2.4All bodywork edges that could contact a pedestrian: minimum 3mm radius
  • T2.2.5Bodywork in front of front wheels: minimum 38mm radius extending at least 45°
  • T2.3.2Minimum static ground clearance (including driver): 30mm
  • T2.4.6Wheel spacers permitted. Laminated or multiple spacers per wheel prohibited
  • T2.5.3Tyre warmers are NOT allowed
  • T2.5.4Special traction agents: PROHIBITED on tyres or track surface
  • T2.6.1Steering using cables or belts: PROHIBITED (except DV autonomous actuators)
  • T2.7.1Minimum wheelbase: 1525mm
Tyre Rules (T2.5)

Dry Tyres

Tyres on the vehicle at technical inspection = defined as dry tyres. Same manufacturer, size and compound on the same axle.

Wet Tyres

Any size/type of treaded or grooved tyre. Grooves must be moulded in by manufacturer (or cut by manufacturer/agent). Minimum tread depth: 2.4mm. One set per type for all dynamic events.

Driver Egress (T4.3 / IN13)
⚠️ Critical Test Requirement
All drivers must be able to exit to the side of the vehicle in no more than 5 seconds. Egress starts with driver fully seated, hands on steering wheel, wearing required driver equipment. Egress stops when both feet are on the ground.

Technical Inspections (Section IN)

Scrutineering sequence — must pass before competing dynamically

🧠 Inspection Order Mnemonic
"Safe Cars Make Every Race Noble"

Safety · Chassis (Mech) · Mech Inspection · EV Battery · Rain Test · Noise & Brake Tests → then Egress → Weigh

Inspection Sequence

Safety Inspection All

Motorsport UK safety inspection. Basic safety equipment check.

Accumulator Inspection EV Only

Battery pack check. Must pass before electrical inspection (IN3).

Electrical Inspection EV Only

Full electrical system review including TS safety circuits (IN4).

Mechanical Inspection All

Chassis, suspension, brakes, bodywork, driver fit (IN5).

Autonomous System Inspection DV Only

AS hardware, software, safety mechanisms (IN6).

Tilt Test All

Vehicle tilted to check for leaks and stability (IN7). CV must pass to be fuelled during events.

Vehicle Weighing All

Weight measured with driver (IN8).

Rain Test EV Only

IP rating / sealing verification for HV components (IN9).

Noise Test All

Maximum permitted noise level checked (IN10).

Brake Test All

All four wheels must lock simultaneously under braking (IN11).

Driver Egress Test All

All drivers must exit in ≤5 seconds. Tallest driver tested first (IN13).

Post-Event Inspection All

After endurance — must pass to score endurance points (IN12).

💡 Key Rule
No approved SES = no dynamic events. SES must be submitted and get 'Approved' before scrutineering. Failed SES can be resubmitted as many times as needed before the resubmission deadline.

Skidpad (D4) — 75 pts

Track Layout

Two pairs of concentric circles, figure-of-eight pattern.
• Circle centres: 18.25m apart
• Inner circle diameter: 15.25m
• Outer circle diameter: 21.25m
• Driving path width: 3m

Procedure

4 initial runs (2 drivers × 2 runs each). Unlimited additional runs after.
Pattern: 1 warm-up lap right → 1 timed right → 1 warm-up left → 1 timed left → exit.
Run time = average of timed left + right + penalties.

💡 Scoring
5 pts guaranteed for completing one run without DNF/DQ. Additional pts if time < Tmax (125% of fastest time).
Acceleration (D5) — 75 pts

Track

75m straight line. At least 3m wide. Cones every ~5m. Standing start (front 0.30m behind starting line).

Top Six Runoff

Top 6 fastest cars qualify. Reversed order start (slowest first). Each driver gets 1 run. Points: 1st=5, 2nd=4, 3rd=3, 4th=2, 5th=1, 6th=0.

💡 Scoring
5 pts guaranteed for completing one run. Additional pts if time < Tmax (150% of fastest time).
Autocross (D6) — 100 pts
  • D6.1Handling track. Straights ≤80m. Constant turns ≤50m diameter. Hairpins ≥9m OD. Slaloms 7.5–12m spacing. Min 3m wide. Length <1.5km
  • D6.2.14 initial runs (2 drivers × 2). Unlimited additional after
  • D6.4.2Additional pts if time < Tmax = 145% of fastest time. Guaranteed 5 pts for finishing
Endurance (D7) — 250 pts + Efficiency 100 pts

Track Specs

Straights ≤80m · Turns ≤50m dia · Hairpins ≥9m OD · Slaloms 9–15m · Min 3m wide
Lap ≈ 1km · Total distance ≈ 22km

Driver Change

Mandatory at midpoint (11km each).
Only 3 team members (incl driver) in change area.
EV: one must be an ESO.
Time: 3 min to change driver + 1 min to restart.
Excess time added to final time.

💡 Endurance Scoring
25 pts guaranteed for finishing. Additional pts if time < Tmax (145% of fastest). Vehicle must pass post-event inspection (IN12) to score. Vehicles off-pace (>145% fastest lap) must exit immediately.

Quick Quiz — Test Your Knowledge

Reveal each answer only after you've thought about it first

How to Use
Read each question, answer mentally, then click "Reveal Answer" to check. Works best as a daily review.
1. What is the minimum wheelbase required for all Formula Student vehicles?
1525mm — Rule T2.7.1
2. A driver fails to exit the vehicle in time during egress testing. How many seconds is the limit?
5 seconds — IN13.2.7. Timer starts when driver is seated, hands on wheel; stops when both feet on ground.
3. What is the maximum achievable score in FS Class, and what event is worth the most points?
Total = 1000 points. Largest event = Endurance at 250 points (25% of total). Engineering Design is the highest static event at 150 pts.
4. What is the penalty for arguing with a competition official?
Immediate Disqualification from the competition — Rule A3.3.1. No second chances.
5. What happens to an EV team if one driver's alcohol level is above 0.0%? What if a second team member fails?
First violation: that member is immediately DQ'd for the rest of the competition. Second violation by another team member: entire team is immediately disqualified — A6.6.2.
6. What is the BPP presentation time? What happens if you go over or under?
Presentation must be 9–11 minutes. Under 9 min or over 11 min = penalty. Judges will stop any presentation exceeding 11 minutes — S2.3.4.
7. Name the 4 powertrain types available in the Lap Time Simulation event.
1) NA Gasoline, 2) Turbo-Charged Gasoline, 3) Single Motor EV, 4) Four Motor EV — S7.3.3
8. What are the main hoop minimum wall thickness and cross-sectional area requirements for steel tubes?
Main/Front Hoops: min CSA = 173mm², min wall = 2.0mm, min I = 11320mm⁴ — T3.2.1
9. During endurance, how long does a team have to complete a driver change, and to restart the car?
3 minutes to change driver + 1 minute to restart. Excess time is added to total endurance time — D7.5.4.
10. What is the Tmax formula for the Endurance event? And for Autocross?
Endurance: Tmax = 145% of fastest team's time. Autocross: Tmax = 145% of fastest time. Skidpad: 125%. Acceleration: 150%.
11. What is the cone DOO penalty in Skidpad vs Endurance?
Skidpad: +0.2s per cone. Endurance: +2s per cone. All other events (Accel, Autocross, Trackdrive) = +2s — D9.1.7.
12. A vehicle is covered by a screen in the paddock during the day. What rule is this, and what's the penalty?
Rule A3.9 — Car covering is unsportsmanlike conduct. Penalty: up to –50 points at the Chief Judge's discretion. Exception: overnight cover, rain, tyre debris covers, and severely damaged cars/parts are permitted.
13. What is the minimum tread depth for wet tyres?
2.4mm minimum tread depth — T2.5.1(ii)
14. What three CO₂ conversion factors are used for Efficiency scoring?
E10 Gasoline = 799g CO₂/litre · Electric = 143g CO₂/kWh · E85 = 571g CO₂/litre — D7.9.2
15. How many pages can an EDR (Engineering Design Report) have, and how many must be drawings?
Total max = 8 pages: max 5 pages of content (text/graphs/images) + exactly 3 pages of drawings (front, top, side — one each) — S5.2.2 & S5.2.3
